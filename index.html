History of Japan

Human habitation in Japan can be traced all the way back to prehistoric times.The first known reference was recorded in the Chinese Book of Han. In 784 a new capital was made, Heian-ky≈ç, modern day Kyoto. This was the beginning of the Heian period, which lasted until 1185. The Hein period was the golden age of classical Japanese culture. From this period on the Japanese religious culture was a mix of Buddhism and native religious practices known as Shinto.

Religion in modern day Japan

Shinto and Buddhism are Japan's two major religions. Shinto is as old as Japenese culture. Buddhism was brought to Japan from the mainland during the 6th century. Shinto today is a japanese term that applies to the religion of public shrines devoted to the worship of a number of gods(aka Kami). Shinto is the largest religion in Japan. About 80% of the population practices Shinto, only a small amount of japanese identify themselves as shintoists. Buddhism has been practiced in Japan since its introduction in 552 A.D.. In 2008 about 34% of japanese identified as Buddhists and the number has been growing since 1990.
