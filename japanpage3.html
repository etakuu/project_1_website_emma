Anime & Manga

The start of anime

Anime was first intoduuced in the beggining of the 20th century. The very first generation of animators where reffered to as the "fathers" of anime. They made their first animation in the late 1910s. In the 1980s anime was considered "mainstream" in Japan. The rise of Gundam Macross, Dragon Ball, Real Robot, and CyberPunk genres boomed. In 2001 the movie Spirited Away won the first prize at the Berlin Film Festival in 2002. It also won the 2003 Academy Award for best Animated feature. And in 2004, Innocence: Ghost in the Shell was featured at the Cannes Film Festival.

The start of Manga

Manga is a Japanese comic book and graphic novel. Manga was first developed in the late 19th century. In Japan readers of manga range from little kids to adults. Most manga is aimed at adult audiences. Since 1950s manga has steadily become a major part of the Japanese publishing industry. A manga artist is called a Mangaka. A Mangaka usually works with a few assistants in a small studio and is associated with a creative editor from a commercial publishing company. If a manga becomes popular enough it may be turned into an Anime. Sometimes mangas may be based off of popular Animes as well.
